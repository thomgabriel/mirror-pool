> effective-k over nominal-k, the ~19-participant/tx Solana ceiling, and silent reward accrual — are

## Executive summary

Across six fact-checked research fronts, the state of the art in on-chain privacy converges on one lesson that both validates and stress-tests mirror-pool: an anonymity set is only as strong as its weakest behavioural tell, so the number that matters is the effective anonymity set (entropy over the adversary's posterior), not the nominal head-count the on-chain k-floor enforces. mirror-pool's uniform-actor round is a genuine structural advance — one vault-signed batch (one signer, gas price, timestamp, wallet) neutralises the gas, address-reuse, FIFO-timing and wallet-fingerprint heuristics that deanonymise ~29-44% of Tornado Cash despite sound proofs, and it removes the addressable target Solana's validator-level copy-trade/front-run economy needs. Production precedent exists for the core mechanic (Penumbra's per-block batch DEX; CoW Protocol's uniform-clearing-price settle()), for silent reward accrual (Penumbra's exchange-rate model, the antidote to Tornado's deanonymizing anonymity-mining), and for token-free durable crowds (Privacy Pools) — but three of the most-cited "privacy" mechanisms are less mature than they look: Penumbra's per-contribution amount-hiding (flow encryption) is specced-not-shipped, Token-2022 confidential transfers have been disabled mainnet-wide since June 2025, and Arcium is permissioned mainnet-alpha. The single sharpest finding is that the k-floor as written is a liveness gate, not yet a privacy guarantee: a whale can satisfy k with k self-intents and collapse effective anonymity to 1, so the floor needs per-intent Sybil pricing and a notion of distinct funding sources, and the true guarantee must be measured off-chain by an adversarial harness reporting effective-k and per-participant Bayes vulnerability (<= 1/k). The residual attribution surface the uniform actor cannot fix is entirely pre-round (funding topology) and off-chain (coordinator-submission metadata), which production Sybil pipelines already exploit at scale. On Solana specifically the binding constraint is not cryptography but the per-transaction envelope (64 account locks bind before the 1.4M-CU / ~dozen-proof limit), capping a round at ~19 participants per tx (3 accounts/participant in the real `execute_round`) and ~95 under Jito's 5-tx atomicity, which forces a chunked, resumable, logically-atomic round executor modelled on SPL Stake Pool. For the deferred incentive module the research is unambiguous: pay silently via share appreciation realised at the existing spend, never via a public per-user duration/count reward. For behavioural (non-withdrawal) actions the bar is higher than any studied withdrawal mixer, because a stake/swap/vote carries shape entropy fixed denominations never had — making strict on-chain action-bucketing and a cross-round intersection test load-bearing. Net: the architecture is well-aligned with the bounty's "behavioural obscurity" mission and genuinely novel (no live system combines participant unlinkability + batch fairness + a multi-action layer), but the headline claim of defeating modern AI clustering is earned only once the k-floor is Sybil-priced, the harness certifies effective-k, and rewards/disclosure stay silent.

This synthesis pulls six fact-checked research fronts into one decision-useful view for the mirror-pool team. It is written to inform — not change — the roadmap: every finding is framed as an option with tradeoffs, maturity is labelled honestly (production / mainnet-alpha / research / proposed / deprecated-or-defunct), and any claim a fact-checker marked OVERSTATED or UNVERIFIED is carried through as an explicit caveat, never asserted as fact. Citations are inline as primary-source URLs.

## 1. The load-bearing finding: the on-chain k-floor is a liveness gate, not yet a privacy guarantee

Three independent fronts converge on the same structural gap. The k-floor counts *nominal* intents on-chain, but the quantity that resists a 2026 adversary is the *effective* anonymity set — the entropy of the adversary's posterior over "which participant did which action," not the head-count (Serjantov & Danezis, *Towards an Information-Theoretic Metric for Anonymity*, PET 2002, https://www.freehaven.net/anonbib/cache/serjantov02.pdf). Two forces drive nominal-k and effective-k apart:

- **Sybil self-filling.** If one actor supplies k−1 of k intents, the single honest participant's effective anonymity collapses to 1 — a definitional property of anonymity sets, not a novel result (Quintyne-Collins, *Characterizing Sybil Attacks in Cryptocurrency Mixers*, https://eprint.iacr.org/2019/1111; Bissias, https://people.cs.umass.edu/~gbiss/mixing.pdf; 2025 mixing SoK, https://arxiv.org/pdf/2504.20296). As written the k-floor does not price this, so it functions as a liveness gate. The standard countermeasure is a per-intent economic cost (fee/bond) plus, ideally, counting *distinct funding sources* rather than raw intents. [research; recommendation: investigate — the "k-floor is a liveness gate" line is our own analysis, not a sourced claim.]
- **Post-heuristic shrinkage.** Every deployed ZK mixer leaks well below its advertised set from timing/metadata alone, despite sound proofs. Tutela flagged ~29% of the 10-ETH Tornado pool (8,060 / 28,188 deposits) as heuristically compromised (https://arxiv.org/abs/2201.06811); Wang et al. (WWW'23) show five heuristics materially raise depositor↔withdrawer linkage (https://dl.acm.org/doi/10.1145/3543507.3583217). (An early 2025 cross-chain preprint (arXiv:2510.09433) attached a specific FIFO-lift figure but was withdrawn 2025-11-18 for reference errors — cite the direction, not a borrowed digit; the load-bearing claim rests on the non-withdrawn Tutela/Wang primaries.)

The implication is not "the k-floor is wrong" — it is that the k-floor guarantees N≥k *candidates*, and only Sybil-pricing plus a near-uniform posterior make effective-k ≈ k. Tutela's own method (true set = nominal − heuristically-linkable, https://github.com/pareto-xyz/tutela-app) is the template, and the sharp architectural point is that the program can only count *nominal* intents on-chain — the true-set computation is inherently an off-chain harness + client responsibility. Three non-exclusive options: (a) price each intent (bond / fee / bucketed-minimum) so faking k costs more than attracting k; (b) surface a "true k" to a user before they commit, with opt-out when it is too low; (c) build the adversarial harness (§6) that reports effective-k and per-participant Bayes vulnerability and *fails* any round whose true set < k even when nominal ≥ k.

## 2. What the uniform-actor design already wins — and the two surfaces it doesn't

The single vault-signed batch is a real structural advantage over per-user mixers, and it is where mirror-pool's novelty lives. Because one signer, one fee-payer, one gas price, one timestamp and one wallet execute for all k participants, the round *by construction* neutralises the on-chain half of the production adversary: the gas-price fingerprint, address-reuse at the action site, the FIFO-at-execution tell, and the wallet-software fingerprint — exactly the quasi-identifiers that break Tornado (Beres et al., https://arxiv.org/abs/2005.14051; Tutela). It also removes the addressable target Solana's copy-trade/front-run economy needs: MEV there has moved from a (deprecated) mempool to validator-level shred-streaming, and "wide" multi-slot sandwiches rose from ~1% to ~30% of sandwiches while per-wallet copy-trade bots became mass-consumer (Ghostlogs @ Accelerate 2025, https://solanacompass.com/learn/accelerate-25/scale-or-die-at-accelerate-2025-the-state-of-solana-mev). With no per-participant pending transaction, "front-run wallet X / copy-trade wallet Y" has no target once an intent enters a round.

Two surfaces remain, neither fixable purely on-chain:
- **Pre-round funding topology.** Production clustering treats "who first funded this wallet" as the most reliable account-model signal (practitioner writeup, https://airdropfarming.org/blog/behavioural-fingerprints-chain-analysis-uses-to-cluster-wallets — blog-grade, but corroborated by Chainalysis's funding rollup and Trusta's "gas-provision-network" graph, https://github.com/TrustaLabs/Airdrop-Sybil-Identification). If several participants funded their note-wallets from a correlated source or in a tight window, an analyst clusters the *deposit* side even though execution is uniform. These pipelines run at scale (LayerZero's 2024 sweep confirmed 803,093 Sybil addresses, https://crypto.news/layerzero-spots-800k-sybil-addresses-airdrop-scheme/).
- **Post-round settlement + coordinator-submission metadata.** Where behavioural outputs land, and the network-layer timing/ordering of intent submission to the liveness-only coordinator, are attribution channels the on-chain program never sees.

**Novelty check (favourable for the bounty):** no live Solana protocol implements an on-chain k-floor synchronized-round model — Privacy Cash validates the exact Circom/Groth16/Anchor stack in production but is individual-action with no round or k-floor (https://github.com/Privacy-Cash/privacy-cash). And no production or academic system combines (a) participant unlinkability, (b) batch price/MEV fairness, and (c) a generic multi-action layer — every precedent does at most two (Tornado/Railgun do (a); CoW does (b) with fully public participants). Good for novelty scoring, but it means there is no existing playbook for k-sizing or round cadence for *this* combination.

## 3. Front 1 — Incentives & crowd depth

Badly-shaped incentives deanonymise faster than they deepen. The canonical footgun is Tornado Cash "anonymity mining," which paid a fixed, public Anonymity-Point reward per block per deposit — so the AP a user later claims reveals exactly how many blocks their deposit sat, re-linking deposit to withdrawal (Tornado docs, https://github.com/tornadocash/docs/blob/en/tornado-cash-classic/anonymity-mining.md; Tutela's "TORN mining" heuristic). [deprecated; program ran 18 Dec 2020 – 18 Dec 2021.] The durable pattern is the opposite — pay *silently*: Penumbra accrues staking yield into a staking-token↔delegation-token *exchange rate* that appreciates, so a delegator earns continuously-compounded reward over exactly their delegation period with no per-delegator reward event and no claim transaction (https://guide.penumbra.zone/overview/staking; spec https://protocol.penumbra.zone/main/index.html). [production — private PoS mainnet is live; the mechanism is unchanged from the testnet-era write-up sometimes cited, so cite the guide/spec as load-bearing.]

Liveness is funded not by inflationary deposit rewards but by permissionless, bonded, fee-paid relayer/broadcaster markets: Tornado's registry required a 300-TORN stake and took a 0.3% fee to listed relayers; Railgun's broadcasters charge ~10% over gas, require no RAIL token, and are provably non-custodial — the contract reverts on any change and the broadcaster cannot decrypt the transaction (https://docs.railgun.org/wiki/learn/privacy-system/community-broadcasters). [production.] Two token-free existence proofs matter: Privacy Pools sustains a compliant crowd via association-set/access utility with no yield token (https://papers.ssrn.com/sol3/papers.cfm?abstract_id=4563364; 0xbow mainnet launch 31 Mar 2025, https://www.theblock.co/post/348959/0xbow-privacy-pools-new-cypherpunk-tool-inspired-research-ethereum-founder-vitalik-buterin) — though "deep, long-lived crowd" *overstates* current adoption (~$6M / ~1,500 users is modest; the incentive-design lesson holds regardless). Cold-start is addressed by anchor-tenant seeding (ORE seeded Privacy Cash's pool — secondary-sourced, so the $150M / 14-audit figures want primary confirmation) plus shield-by-default UX (Zcash shielded supply grew ~11%→~30% in 2025, https://coinmetrics.substack.com/p/state-of-the-network-issue-338 — but Coin Metrics attributes this "primarily [to] moving value into/out of the shielded pool," a partial-shielded-flow nuance to a pure "shield-by-default deepened privacy" read).

**Options for mirror-pool:**
- *Incentive module (deferred): adopt silent accrual, avoid public per-user meters.* Pay any fee-reward or stake yield by appreciating a pooled note/commitment share realised at the existing Groth16/Poseidon spend — never a per-participant reward event, claim tx, or duration/count meter. Fund it from submission/round fees (a Sybil tax that doubles as liveness collateral), not a new inflationary token.
- *k-floor: investigate Sybil pricing* (see §1): per-intent bond/fee + distinct-funding-source counting.
- *Bootstrapping: adapt anchor-tenant seeding + default-to-pooled UX*, but label it honestly — seed/treasury intents buy *liveness, not anonymity*, and may be discountable by an analyst if on-chain-distinguishable from honest intents (open question).
- *Opt-in disclosure: investigate association-set proofs over viewing keys.* A subset-membership proof ("my action was within the compliant set") is cleaner than handing out viewing keys — but confirm it composes with the round model rather than leaking which crowd a participant was in.

## 4. Front 2 — Timing, batching & correlation resistance

The state of the art: settle many actions as one indistinguishable aggregate on a *data-independent schedule*, and never let an individual action's moment or content leak before the aggregate is fixed. Penumbra's shielded DEX is the closest production analog to synchronized rounds — every swap for a pair in a ~5s block clears at one uniform price with no attacker-manipulable intra-block ordering (https://guide.penumbra.zone/dex; https://protocol.penumbra.zone/main/dex.html). **Maturity nuance (fact-check: OVERSTATED):** on today's mainnet individual swap *input* amounts are still public — per-contribution amount-hiding ("flow encryption") is explicitly a *future upgrade*, not shipped, pending ABCI 2.0 (https://protocol.penumbra.zone/main/dex.html). So Penumbra proves *batch execution at a uniform price* in production, but does **not** yet prove production *amount-hiding aggregation*. This is reassuring for us: the deferred piece (a DKG + homomorphic threshold-decryption committee) is exactly what mirror-pool sidesteps by construction — the coordinator never sees plaintext intents (client-side proving + committed bucketed amounts) and the vault is the sole on-chain actor, so the program can sum already-committed bucketed amounts directly instead of adding a decryption committee.

Equal denominations alone do not stop behavioural/timing linkage (Tutela ~29% of the 10-ETH pool; Beres et al. add time-of-day and gas-price profiling, https://arxiv.org/abs/2005.14051). Mixnet research contributes two more primitives: independent per-hop exponential (Poisson) delays that decorrelate input/output timing, and loop/cover traffic that keeps the set full and hides *when* a real action occurs (Loopix, USENIX Security 2017, https://arxiv.org/abs/1703.00536; productionised as Nym since Apr 2022, https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nym_(mixnet)). Recent ML attacks temper the mixnet claims — deep-learning flow-matching still links *some* traffic (MixMatch, PoPETs 2024, https://petsymposium.org/popets/2024/popets-2024-0050.php; its live true-positive rate ~0.26 at FPR 1e-2, much worse than idealised), so timing defences must be *layered*, not relied on singly. Threshold-encrypted mempools attack from the other side — proposers commit to order while contents are encrypted, decrypting only after ordering (Shutter, live on Gnosis Chain, https://blog.shutter.network/applied-mev-protection-via-shutters-threshold-encryption/; batched threshold encryption decrypts a whole block in O(n) ≈ 80 bytes/member, USENIX Security 2024, https://eprint.iacr.org/2024/669 — *research, unaudited*, and it is the load-bearing "decrypt k as one round" piece, so not production).

**Options for mirror-pool:**
- *Coordinator timing: adopt fixed-epoch, data-independent cadence.* Fire rounds on a fixed slot cadence once the k-floor is met; if k is unmet at the boundary, roll intents to the next epoch rather than firing early. A *reactive* trigger ("execute the moment the k-th intent lands") leaks the marginal participant — the boundary timestamp correlates to the last submitter. (Open risk: rolling unmet intents forward may create its own age-based correlation signal.)
- *Thin rounds: adapt pool-generated decoy/cover intents.* Vault self-directed, bucketed, no-op/self-cancelling actions that are on-chain-indistinguishable from real intents top a round to k and blur true count/timing — the behavioural analog of Loopix loop traffic. (Open economics: who funds decoy fees/stake costs, and can decoys be made truly indistinguishable on Solana? Couples to the incentive module.)
- *Coordinator "sees who→what" gap: investigate light commit-reveal first.* Hash-committed intents revealed only at round assembly let the coordinator order/commit before it can read shapes — cheap, no committee, keeps the "coordinator holds no keys" invariant. Reserve Shutter-style threshold encryption for later hardening; a keyper committee adds a liveness/trust dependency that conflicts with the liveness-only, no-keys design.
- *Per-hop jitter: investigate as a network-layer second layer only.* On Solana's ~400 ms slots the round-boundary cadence is the primary timing defence; bounded submission jitter is complementary, not a substitute.

## 5. Front 3 — Behavioral action pooling (one actor for many)

"One actor executes for many" splits into three families. **Live, action-specific pooling:** CoW Protocol's single `settle()` atomically fills every order in a batch at a uniform clearing price — "every user receives the same price for the same token" (https://docs.cow.fi/cow-protocol/reference/contracts/core/settlement; GPv2Settlement verified at 0x9008D19f58AAbD9eD0D60971565AA8510560ab41). This is the closest production validation of the core round mechanic and the direct template for a future Swap adapter: route a round's swaps to clear at the round's one realised fill, never a per-member individually-timed swap whose slippage fingerprints order size and position. Penumbra pools staking with the vault-as-sole-delegator shape (https://protocol.penumbra.zone/main/stake.html) — **fact-check nuance (OVERSTATED):** the *encrypted-aggregate* delegation-amount privacy depends on the same deferred flow-encryption, so it is specced-not-live; the *delegation-token + shielded-pool + exchange-rate reward* parts are live and are what we actually want to borrow. (Correcting a bad caveat in the source material: Penumbra did **not** wind down — an April 2025 post announced only a leadership change, stating "the protocol is unaffected," https://hdevalence.ca/blog/2025-04-15-penumbra-labs/.)

**Thin-adapter ancestors:** Aztec Connect is the closest architectural ancestor of the PooledAction trait itself — a ~50–100-line per-protocol bridge through which one aggregated L1 call executed up to 896 users' proved intents, splitting gas and proceeds pro-rata into fresh private notes (https://aztec.network/blog/private-defi-with-the-aztec-connect-bridge). [deprecated: deposits stopped Mar 2023, sequencer Mar 2024.] Adopt the shape; **avoid its failure mode.** On shutdown Aztec Labs *renounced all admin keys* on the immutable rollup contract; ~3 years later an attacker drained ~$2.1M via a proof-verification/settlement mismatch (the proof accepts a 32-row group but settlement acted only on the first `numRealTxs`), with no one able to intervene (https://thedefiant.io/news/hacks/aztec-connect-deprecated-contract-exploit-2-1m-zk-proof; https://aztec-labs.com/blog/aztec-connect-incident.html). This is the concrete argument for keeping a *guarded, ideally time-locked* Squads-multisig upgrade authority plus a credible exit/withdraw path, rather than trustlessly abandoning the program. ERC-7683 is a useful outside check on the trait's *extensibility* — a fixed settler interface plus typed, versioned, off-chain-registered `orderData` sub-types (https://www.erc7683.org/spec) — but it is **Draft, not ratified** (https://eips.ethereum.org/EIPS/eip-7683); borrow the pattern (model each adapter's `Params` as a typed versioned payload so `execute_round`'s CPI dispatch stays generic), not the spec.

**Research / negative signals:** Anoma's solver model is the generalised end-state of "k intents → one settlement," but only its token/governance layer is live (Sept 2025); the intent-netting core is pre-deployment (https://anoma.net/blog/xan-is-live) — study it only if/when a round should *net* same-round buy/sell or LP-in/out before touching an external venue. SUAVE (Flashbots) is a **cautionary negative:** a well-funded attempt to build confidential-compute infra so a third party could act on unseen intents was archived 12 May 2025 without reaching sustained production (https://github.com/flashbots/suave-geth). This *validates* keeping the coordinator liveness-only and cryptography-blind — resist any future temptation to route Swap/LP intents through richer coordinator-side decryption "for better netting." Jito bundles are relevant only to round-*submission* hardening (§7), not to pooling identity.

## 6. Front 4 — Measuring anonymity (the harness and the metric)

This front is effectively the spec for the anonymity-test harness. **The metric.** Report the entropy of the adversary's posterior over "which participant did which action," not the nominal k. **Attribution caveat (fact-check: OVERSTATED):** Serjantov & Danezis (PET 2002, https://www.freehaven.net/anonbib/cache/serjantov02.pdf) define the *effective size* as the entropy H itself (in bits) — the "2^H" form (equivalent number of equiprobable users / perplexity) is a valid *later* adaptation, not S&D's definition, so attribute it as such. The complementary normalised degree d = H/H_max is from the *separate* Diaz–Seys–Claessens–Preneel PET 2002 paper (https://www.freehaven.net/anonbib/cache/THV04.pdf). Pair the average-case entropy with the conservative worst case: min-entropy / Bayes vulnerability V = max_i p_i — the single-guess success probability of a computationally-unbounded adversary — which is exactly the quantity to bound at ≤ 1/k (Smith, *On the Foundations of Quantitative Information Flow*, FoSSaCS 2009, https://cs.au.dk/~askarov/lbs-course/2025/reading/qif.pdf — note the often-cited "Quantifying Information Flow Using Min-Entropy" is the QEST 2011 paper, not the 2009 origin). Reporting only Shannon would mask a round where one participant is highly exposed while the average looks safe; min-entropy catches exactly that.

**The battery.** Build the harness around the *real* chain-analysis heuristics, not naive randomness: address-reuse, unique-gas-price, linked-address, multi-denomination and anonymity-mining-claim (Tutela's five Tornado reveals, https://arxiv.org/abs/2201.06811), plus FIFO temporal matching and funding-graph clustering. Wang et al. (WWW'23) quantify the stakes — five heuristics materially raise correct depositor-linkage (https://dl.acm.org/doi/10.1145/3543507.3583217). The uniform-actor batch neutralises the gas, wallet-fingerprint and FIFO-at-execution heuristics *by construction* (one signer/gas/timestamp/wallet), but the harness must still exercise the *funding* side and the *settlement* side, where reuse/self-transfer/denomination linkage still bites. Compute the *true* set Tutela-style (nominal − heuristically-linkable, https://github.com/pareto-xyz/tutela-app) and report a *per-participant* conservative score, not one flattering pool-level number — mirroring Wasabi's move from anonymity-*set* to anonymity-*score* (https://blog.wasabiwallet.io/what-is-the-difference-between-an-anonymity-set-and-an-anonymity-score/; note both major CoinJoin coordinators are now defunct — zkSNACKs discontinued Wasabi's coordinator 1 Jun 2024 — so borrow the *metric*, not the service).

Two regimes the cited attacks do **not** cover, which the harness must add:
- *Behavioural (non-withdrawal) actions carry shape entropy a fixed-denomination withdrawal never had* (target protocol, amount, validator, vote choice). Every cited attack targets withdrawal mixers; the (participant→action) posterior over non-interchangeable actions is empirically unstudied. This makes strict standardized action buckets (bucketed stake sizes, whitelisted targets, quantized vote weights) load-bearing, and the harness must assert the emitted batch is byte-uniform across participants per adapter.
- *Cross-round intersection.* "Longevous crowds" cut both ways — a participant recurring across many rounds may become *more* linkable via set-intersection even if each round is individually sound. None of the per-round audits model this; the harness needs a multi-round simulation, not just per-round checks.

**Options:** adopt the entropy + min-entropy metrics and the heuristic battery as the harness spec; adopt standardized/byte-uniform action shapes; treat the reward-claim graph as an *additional* harness heuristic (§3); and decide which prior the harness certifies against (a behaviourally-weighted prior — some participants are known whales/MMs — is lower and more honest than a uniform prior, and materially changes the claim).

## 7. Front 5 — Solana privacy primitives & the resource envelope

The binding constraint on a k=hundreds round is not cryptography but Solana's per-transaction ceilings: **64 account locks** (the 128 bump via `increase_tx_account_lock_limit` is *not* live — the feature gate shows no mainnet activation epoch, https://github.com/solana-labs/solana/issues/27241; treat 64 as effective), **1.4M compute units**, and a **1,232-byte** message (https://solana.com/docs/core/transactions). Groth16 verification via alt_bn128 is cheap enough to be viable — Light Protocol's groth16-solana benchmarks 78,293 CU (1 public input) to 108,762 CU (8 inputs) (https://github.com/Lightprotocol/groth16-solana) — — but note the CU is not even paid here: in mirror-pool the Groth16 verify happens once per intent in `commit_intent`, not in `execute_round`, whose per-participant work is just lamport transfers. The **64-account lock binds first**: the real `ExecuteRound` has 6 fixed accounts plus **3 per participant** (`intent`, `recipient`, `relayer` — the nullifier is settled earlier in `commit_intent`, not here), so one instruction realistically caps at **~19 participants** (verified against the merged code, not the ~2/participant estimate this survey originally assumed). True cross-transaction atomicity tops out at **5 sequential transactions** via a Jito bundle (https://docs.jito.wtf/lowlatencytxnsend/), i.e. **~95 participants** under hard all-or-nothing atomicity. Beyond that a round cannot be one atomic unit and must borrow the **chunked-sequential pattern SPL Stake Pool already uses in production** to update hundreds of validator accounts ("it is impossible to update the total value of the stake pool in one instruction… the command-line utility breaks up transactions," https://github.com/solana-labs/solana-program-library/blob/master/docs/src/stake-pool/overview.md — note this is a *client-side* chunking convention, so mirror-pool's *on-chain* executor needs its own resumable design).

Two native privacy primitives are notably immature/offline and should **not** be designed against for the withdraw-only core. Token-2022 confidential transfers have been **disabled network-wide since 19 Jun 2025** after a Fiat-Shamir transcript soundness bug, with no confirmed re-enablement as of this research (https://solana.com/news/post-mortem-june-25-2025; https://github.com/solana-program/token-2022/issues/657) — a double lesson that (i) "audited" ≠ sound (a multiply-audited primitive shipped a critical soundness bug that lived ~a year), so mirror-pool's circuit and verifier need independent audit before mainnet, and (ii) a single feature-gate flip took every dependent app offline overnight, and mirror-pool's own dependence on alt_bn128 syscalls carries the same systemic class of risk — name it as an accepted risk, not an implicit assumption. Arcium's MPC network is **mainnet-alpha / permissioned / early-stage** (fact-check correction to a "production" label), self-described as running "with some centralized components" (https://blockeden.xyz/blog/2026/02/12/arcium-mainnet-alpha-encrypted-supercomputer-solana/) — a *future-only* candidate if the deferred incentive module ever needs the coordinator to compute over intents it cannot currently see; YAGNI says not now. Privacy Cash (https://github.com/Privacy-Cash/privacy-cash) is the closest live analog and **de-risks the Circom/Groth16/Anchor stack choice**, but has no round/k-floor — confirming the round design is a genuine ecosystem gap.

**Options:** adopt the resource envelope as the literal round-batching unit (size chunks to ~20–25; expect N chunk-txs at k=hundreds); adapt the SPL-Stake-Pool chunked pattern into a **resumable round-state PDA** that accumulates chunk commitments and is *sealed* (action goes live) only once the k-floor is met before a deadline slot, with an explicit unwind/refund path otherwise — a first-order round-executor decision, since beyond 5 chunk-txs you cannot rely on hard Solana atomicity and need this *logical* atomicity; adapt Light Protocol's async nullifier-queue/forester pattern *only if* many chunks must land in the same slot (they otherwise serialize under the per-account write-lock), while never citing ZK Compression as a privacy primitive (it is a state-cost tool; compressed data stays publicly readable, https://github.com/Lightprotocol/light-protocol/blob/main/light-paper.md). Direct checks worth doing: whether the 64→128 gate has since activated; whether the ZK ElGamal Proof program has been re-enabled; and a CU benchmark of mirror-pool's *own* circuit (its public-input count may differ from the 1–8 benchmarked range).

## 8. Front 6 — The adversary: production chain-analysis & MEV

The adversary is not hypothetical. Chainalysis and Elliptic both ship **Solana-account-model-specific** clustering today — Chainalysis rolls Token/Stake Accounts back to their System Account (SPL ownership changes in <1% of transfers, NFT >20%, and a naive cluster makes a new owner "inherit" the account's whole history, https://www.chainalysis.com/blog/solana-chainalysis/), and Elliptic's Dec 2025 platform "automatically links main and token accounts" (https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/elliptic-launches-next-generation-platform-to-power-the-future-of-digital-asset-forensics-and-compliance-302637723.html). This is directly load-bearing for the stake/swap adapters: **any vault-created sub-account (stake account, ATA) must have the vault as its sole on-chain authority — never a participant-derived seed or authority — or ownership-rollup logic re-individuates participants inside an otherwise-uniform round.** Because Solana is account-based, the UTXO common-input-ownership heuristic does not transfer literally; the operative signals are funding topology, timing correlation, and interaction-sequence fingerprints — the same feature set production AI/ML Sybil pipelines run at scale (Trusta Labs' graph-community-detection + K-means, https://github.com/TrustaLabs/Airdrop-Sybil-Identification; LayerZero's 803,093-address sweep). *(The one cited UTXO statistic — 184M→40M Bitcoin clusters — is **UNVERIFIED** (single Substack, no primary) and should be dropped; the CIOH + Union-Find *methodology* is textbook-production and the transferable principle is "things authorized/paid together are assumed co-owned" — a failure mode a poorly-laid-out batch tx could reintroduce via per-user fee top-ups or CPI account ordering that leak which account-slice belongs to which intent.)*

Most load-bearing: **ZK shielded pools are not immune.** Preprints deanonymise 21–35% of Tornado withdrawals and report comparable Railgun leakage purely from timing/address-reuse/metadata, despite sound proofs — with FIFO nearest-in-time matching the cheap high-yield attack (https://arxiv.org/html/2510.09433v1; Railgun *A Tattered Cloak of Invisibility*, https://arxiv.org/pdf/2606.25926). **Maturity honesty:** both are arXiv *preprints* (the source's "peer-reviewed" wording is not verified), and one fact-check front flagged the Tornado preprint's status as unstable — so treat the *direction* (ZK pools leak from timing/metadata) as well-corroborated by the SUPPORTED Tutela/Wang primaries and the *specific* 21–35% as indicative. The design consequence: proof soundness buys *zero* protection against timing/metadata correlation, so k-floor enforcement + round-timing discipline are where the real anonymity budget is spent, and the note-commit→next-intent time-gap is a security parameter *across* rounds, not just within one.

On front-running/copy-trading the uniform-actor thesis removes the target at the source (§2). The residual risk moves up one level: the vault's *own* settlement transaction for a future Swap/LP adapter is itself one large visible order a searcher could sandwich like any big trade — so a swap/LP adapter needs its own batch-level MEV defence (CoW's uniform-clearing-price batch is the proven production precedent, https://docs.cow.fi/cow-protocol/concepts/benefits/mev-protection; **investigate** submitting the round tx via a Jito private-relay path to shrink the pre-landing censor/front-run window, weighed against Jito's dominant validator-client share vs the permissionless-coordinator goal). Finally, a principle close to a restatement of the bounty brief: **naive per-user randomization is itself statistically distinguishable** — uniform jitter produces a flat distribution unlike organic bimodal/log-normal behaviour (practitioner argument, https://airdropfarming.org/blog/gas-pattern-obfuscation-why-your-sybil-wallets-look-identical-to-chain-analysis — *proposed*, blog-grade, treat prescriptions as opinion; the underlying anomaly-detection principle is standard). This is the strongest argument for the actual architecture over any "add noise per user" alternative: collapsing k participants into one uniform actor means there is *no per-user distribution to test*. The residual obligation is ensuring the k-floor, bucket-selection and round cadence don't quietly reintroduce a testable regularity.

## 9. Decision points the roadmap will have to settle

These are the sharpest cross-front open questions, framed as decisions (not mandates):
- **Does the k-floor stay a nominal count, or gain Sybil pricing + distinct-funding-source counting?** The difference between "k participated" and "k-anonymous." (Fronts 1, 4)
- **What minimum k is defensible against modern ML clustering?** No surveyed source gives a first-principles number; small equal-denomination sets degrade fast (~29–44% compromised). Consider whether the floor should *scale with observed behavioural entropy* rather than being a fixed constant. (Fronts 2, 4, 6)
- **How is the round triggered?** Fixed-cadence firing with roll-over avoids leaking the marginal participant — but does the age of rolled-over intents become its own signal? (Front 2)
- **Commit-reveal vs threshold encryption for the intent pool** — the light option closes the "coordinator sees who→what" gap without a committee and preserves the no-keys invariant; threshold encryption is a heavier, later option that adds a liveness dependency. (Front 2)
- **Reward accrual mechanism** — silent exchange-rate/share appreciation realised at spend (no claim tx) is the research-backed choice; it needs new state (e.g. a per-validator exchange-rate account for a Stake adapter) not in the current design. (Fronts 1, 3)
- **Round scaling** — how many chunk-txs a k=hundreds round needs, and therefore whether a 5-tx Jito bundle can cover a round atomically or whether the logical-atomicity round-PDA is mandatory. (Front 5)
- **Which prior does the harness certify against?** Uniform is optimistic; a behaviourally-weighted prior (whales/MMs/agents — the bounty's own target users) is more realistic and lowers effective k. (Front 4)
- **Cross-round intersection** — does the harness need a multi-round simulation to certify "longevous crowds" don't become more linkable over time? (Fronts 4, 6)

## Architecture connection map

How each researched practice maps onto a mirror-pool component, grouped by priority. (Advisory — see caveat.)

### high priority

- **on-chain k-floor + anonymity-test harness** ← Sybil-resistant mixing: per-input economic cost/bond so an adversary cannot self-fill the set (eprint 2019/1111; mixing SoK arxiv 2504.20296) *(research (countermeasure proposed, not empirically calibrated))*
  Price each intent with a slashable bond/fee (or a bucketed minimum action size) so satisfying k with self-intents costs strictly more than attracting k real participants, and count distinct funding sources rather than raw intents. Without this the k-floor is a liveness gate, not a privacy guarantee (a whale supplying k-1 intents collapses effective anonymity to 1). Model it explicitly in the harness.
- **anonymity-test harness (the Impact metric)** ← Entropy-based effective anonymity set + min-entropy/Bayes-vulnerability worst case (Serjantov-Danezis PET'02; Diaz et al PET'02; Smith FoSSaCS'09) *(research (foundational metrics))*
  Report per-round posterior entropy H (S&D's effective size in bits; the 2^H 'equivalent equiprobable users' form is a later adaptation, cite it as such), normalised degree d=H/Hmax, AND per-participant Bayes vulnerability V=max p_i, with the pass condition V<=1/k under the strongest heuristic combination. Reporting only Shannon hides a round where one participant is exposed while the average looks safe. Decide the prior (a whale/MM-weighted prior is more honest than uniform and lowers effective k).
- **anonymity-test harness (the battery)** ← Run the real chain-analysis heuristics, not naive randomness: Tutela's 5 Tornado reveals + Beres timing/gas + Wang WWW'23 + FIFO temporal matching + funding-graph clustering *(production (heuristics deployed by Chainalysis/Elliptic/Trusta and academic auditors))*
  Assert no heuristic collapses the (intent->participant) candidate set below k over simulated rounds. The uniform batch kills gas/wallet/FIFO-at-execution by construction, so focus the harness on the funding side and settlement side (reuse/self-transfer/denomination), plus two regimes the literature doesn't cover: heterogeneous behavioural-action shape entropy and cross-round intersection (needs multi-round simulation). Compute a Tutela-style true set = nominal - heuristically-linkable and fail any round whose true set < k.
- **deferred bonding + fee-reward incentive module** ← Silent reward accrual via exchange-rate/share appreciation (Penumbra shielded staking) vs the Tornado anonymity-mining footgun *(production (Penumbra live) / deprecated (Tornado mining = anti-pattern))*
  Accrue any fee-reward or stake yield into a pooled note/commitment share whose value is realised only at the existing Groth16/Poseidon spend - no per-user reward event, no claim tx, no publicly-readable duration/count meter (a public tenure/count reward reconstructs exactly the member->action map the k-floor hides). Fund it from submission/round fees, not a new inflationary deposit-reward token.
- **coordinator round-trigger timing** ← Fixed-epoch, data-independent settlement cadence (Penumbra per-block batch) *(production)*
  Fire rounds on a fixed slot cadence once the on-chain k-floor is met, decoupled from marginal-intent arrival; roll unmet intents to the next epoch rather than firing early. A reactive 'execute on the k-th intent' trigger leaks the marginal participant via the round-boundary timestamp. Watch that rolled-over intent age doesn't become its own correlation signal.
- **round executor / round-batching unit** ← Solana per-transaction envelope: 64 account locks (128 gate not live), 1.4M CU, 1232-byte message; groth16-solana ~78-109k CU/proof *(production)*
  Size each round chunk to ~20-25 participants (2 accounts each + ~8-12 fixed) because the 64-account lock binds well before the ~dozen-proof CU limit. This is the literal envelope the vault-signed instruction must fit; k=hundreds cannot be one instruction. Benchmark mirror-pool's own circuit CU (public-input count differs from the 1-8 benchmarked range).
- **round executor scaling / logical atomicity** ← SPL Stake Pool chunked-sequential pattern + Jito 5-tx atomicity ceiling *(production)*
  True atomicity tops out at 5 bundled txs (~100-125 participants). Beyond that, adapt the stake-pool chunked pattern into a resumable round-state PDA that accumulates chunk commitments and is 'sealed' (action goes live) only once the k-floor is met before a deadline slot, with an explicit unwind/refund path if the floor isn't reached. Note stake-pool chunking is a client-side convention; mirror-pool's on-chain executor needs its own progress-tolerant design.
- **PooledAction stake/swap sub-accounts** ← Production Solana account clustering that rolls Token/Stake Accounts back to a System Account (Chainalysis, Elliptic) *(production)*
  Every vault-created sub-account (stake account, ATA) must have the vault as its sole on-chain authority of record - never a participant-derived seed or authority - or ownership-rollup logic re-individuates participants inside an otherwise-uniform round. Cheap, structural, and closes a concrete production-adversary vector.
- **standardized action shapes (all PooledActions)** ← Fixed bucketed denominations (Tornado/Wasabi/Zcash) + Tutela cross-pool denomination-portfolio-matching leak *(production)*
  Enforce strict coarse buckets on-chain for every PooledAction (withdraw/stake/swap/LP/vote) alongside the k-floor, and assert the emitted batch is byte-uniform across participants per adapter. Treat cross-action portfolio matching (a user touching >1 action type across >=3 rounds) as a first-class threat, since behavioural actions carry far more shape entropy than a fixed withdrawal.
### medium priority

- **coordinator intent pool (who->what gap)** ← Commit-then-reveal / threshold-encrypted mempool (Shutter live on Gnosis; batched threshold encryption USENIX'24) *(production (Shutter) / research (batched TE))*
  Light tier first: users submit hash-committed intents and reveal preimages only at round assembly, so the coordinator orders/commits before it can read shapes - cheap, no committee, preserves the 'coordinator holds no keys' invariant. Reserve Shutter-style threshold encryption for later hardening; a keyper committee adds a liveness/trust dependency that conflicts with the liveness-only design.
- **coordinator round-trigger / k-floor (thin rounds)** ← Loop/cover traffic to keep the set full and hide when a real action occurs (Loopix, productionised as Nym) *(production (via Nym))*
  Let the pool generate decoy intents - vault self-directed, bucketed, no-op/self-cancelling actions that are on-chain-indistinguishable from real intents - to top thin rounds up to k and blur true count/timing. Converts the k-floor from a hard stall into a smoothly satisfiable gate. Open economics: who funds decoy fees/stake costs, and can decoys be made truly unfilterable on Solana (couples to the incentive module).
- **future Swap/LP PooledAction adapter** ← CoW Protocol uniform-clearing-price batch settle(); Penumbra batch swaps *(production)*
  Route a round's aggregated swap intents so every member clears at the round's one realised fill, never a per-member individually-timed swap whose slippage fingerprints order size and batch position. This also gives the vault's own settlement tx a batch-level MEV defence (the residual risk once per-participant txs are gone).
- **PooledAction trait + stake adapter (aggregate-then-one-call)** ← Aztec Connect thin-adapter bridge (aggregate -> one call -> pro-rata new notes); its key-renouncement failure mode *(deprecated (instructive ancestor + cautionary tale))*
  Adopt the shape: a thin per-protocol adapter through which one aggregated CPI executes many proved intents with proceeds re-minted as fresh notes. Avoid the failure mode: keep a guarded, ideally time-locked Squads-multisig upgrade authority and a credible exit/withdraw path rather than renouncing keys - the immutable Aztec contract was drained ~$2.1M three years post-deprecation with no one able to intervene.
- **opt-in viewing-key disclosure** ← Privacy Pools association-set / proof-of-innocence membership proofs (0xbow, Ethereum mainnet) *(production (modest scale, ~$6M / ~1,500 users))*
  Prefer a ZK subset/association-set membership proof ('my action was within the compliant set') over handing out viewing keys - it discloses compliance without revealing the member->action link. Verify it composes with the synchronized-round model and doesn't itself leak which crowd a participant was in. Also a live existence proof that a durable crowd can be sustained by access/compliance utility with no yield token.
- **Merkle/nullifier state at scale** ← Light Protocol concurrent trees + async nullifier-queue/forester pattern (write-lock contention) *(production)*
  Tree depth is not a concern (height-20 vs Light's depth-26). The real lesson is write contention: chunk-txs all appending to the same Merkle-root/nullifier account serialize under the per-account write-lock and can't land in parallel within a slot. If many chunks must land same-slot at higher k, evaluate an async queue-and-drain nullifier pattern before contention binds. Never cite ZK Compression as a privacy primitive - it is a state-cost tool and compressed data stays publicly readable.
- **cold-start / round bootstrapping** ← Anchor-tenant seeding + shield-by-default UX (ORE/Privacy Cash; Zcash/Zashi) *(production)*
  Have a partner protocol or treasury commit standing intents each round so honest users can always reach k, and route integrating protocols through the PooledAction adapters by default. Label honestly: seed intents buy liveness, not anonymity, and may be discountable by an analyst if on-chain-distinguishable from honest intents (open question). The ORE/Privacy Cash $150M/14-audit figures are secondary-sourced; confirm before quoting.
### low priority

- **coordinator round-submission** ← Jito private-relay bundle (atomic, mempool-hidden submission) *(production)*
  Optionally submit the round tx via a Jito private-relay path to shrink the pre-landing window in which a censoring/front-running party can react to 'a round is about to fire'. Solana's single-transaction semantics already give per-chunk atomicity, so Jito isn't needed for correctness. Weigh the privacy gain against relying on Jito's dominant validator-client share vs the permissionless-coordinator goal.
- **PooledAction trait extensibility** ← ERC-7683 typed/versioned, off-chain-registered orderData sub-types (Draft, not ratified) *(Draft standard (production adoption by Across/UniswapX))*
  Model each adapter's Params as a typed, versioned payload only that adapter interprets, so execute_round's CPI-dispatch stays generic and a new Stake/LP/Vote adapter never touches the round engine's core. Borrow the extension pattern, not the still-evolving Draft spec.

## Mission alignment (bounty fit)

Honest read: the architecture is well-aligned with the bounty's "behavioural obscurity" mission and is genuinely novel, but the headline claim of defeating modern AI-powered clustering is not yet earned by the current design as described - it is earned only after three specific gaps close.

Where the research says mirror-pool is ON-mission. (1) The uniform-actor round is a direct structural answer to copy-trading and front-running, which is the mission's sharpest target. Solana MEV has moved to validator-level shred-streaming and mass-consumer per-wallet copy-trade bots, with wide multi-slot sandwiches rising from ~1% to ~30% of sandwiches (Ghostlogs @ Accelerate 2025, https://solanacompass.com/learn/accelerate-25/scale-or-die-at-accelerate-2025-the-state-of-solana-mev); a single vault-signed batch removes the per-participant pending transaction those attacks need as a target. (2) It is genuinely novel: no live Solana protocol implements an on-chain k-floor synchronized-round model (Privacy Cash validates the crypto stack but is individual-action, https://github.com/Privacy-Cash/privacy-cash), and no production or academic system combines participant unlinkability + batch price/MEV fairness + a generic multi-action layer - every precedent does at most two. This maps cleanly to the bounty's "behavioural (non-withdrawal) actions are the novel core." (3) "Not about hiding funds" aligns with the Privacy Pools association-set / compliance-utility model and opt-in disclosure (https://papers.ssrn.com/sol3/papers.cfm?abstract_id=4563364), which is mission-consistent and regulatorily defensible. (4) Collapsing k participants into one uniform actor is precisely the "noise that defeats modern clustering, not naive randomness" the brief demands, because there is no per-user gas/timing/amount distribution left for an analyst to statistically test - the strongest available argument for this architecture over any "add noise per user" alternative (Front 6).

Where the research says mirror-pool RISKS drift, with evidence. (1) The hard guarantee is currently a NOMINAL k count, but the quantity that resists a 2026 adversary is the EFFECTIVE (post-Sybil, post-heuristic) set. ZK pools leak 29-44% from timing/metadata despite sound proofs (Tutela https://arxiv.org/abs/2201.06811; Wang WWW'23 https://dl.acm.org/doi/10.1145/3543507.3583217; cross-chain preprint 21-35% https://arxiv.org/html/2510.09433v1, flagged as indicative). A whale can self-fill k intents and collapse effective anonymity to 1 (eprint 2019/1111). So "defeats modern clustering" is aspirational until the k-floor is Sybil-priced and an adversarial harness certifies effective-k and per-participant Bayes vulnerability <= 1/k. (2) The residual attribution surface the uniform actor cannot touch is entirely pre-round funding topology and off-chain coordinator-submission metadata - exactly what production Sybil pipelines exploit (Trusta https://github.com/TrustaLabs/Airdrop-Sybil-Identification; LayerZero's 803,093-address sweep), and exactly the wallets the bounty targets (whales, MMs, agents). "Longevous crowds" durability therefore depends on client-side funding hygiene and submission-channel privacy that live outside the on-chain core. (3) "Longevous crowds" also cut both ways: a participant recurring across many rounds can become MORE linkable via cross-round set-intersection even if each round is individually sound - a regime none of the cited per-round audits model, so the harness needs multi-round simulation. (4) The deferred incentive module is the single highest-leverage way to accidentally undo the mission: a public duration/count reward reconstructs the member->action map the k-floor hides (Tornado anonymity mining). Research is unambiguous - silent share appreciation only. (5) "Production-grade, scalable" collides with Solana's per-transaction envelope: k=hundreds cannot be one atomic tx and needs a chunked, logically-atomic executor (solvable via the SPL Stake Pool precedent, but must be designed in, not assumed).

Net verdict: the design's structural choices (uniform actor, on-chain k-floor, bucketed shapes, liveness-only crypto-blind coordinator) are the right ones for the mission and are backed by production precedent. The bounty's strongest claim becomes defensible once (a) the k-floor is Sybil-priced against self-filling and ideally counts distinct funding sources, (b) an adversarial heuristic + effective-anonymity harness certifies per-round effective-k, Bayes vulnerability <= 1/k, and cross-round intersection, and (c) reward and disclosure mechanisms stay silent. All three are the specific, research-identified gaps between "k participated" and "k-anonymous against a 2026 adversary" - and none of them requires changing the core architecture, only completing it.

## Appendix A — Gaps for follow-up research

The six fronts thoroughly cover the on-chain anonymity MECHANISM (round/k-floor, batching, action pooling, anonymity measurement, Solana primitives) and its PASSIVE forensic adversary, but a roadmap needs four systematic blind spots that fell between them. (1) The legal/compliance envelope — Privacy Pools association sets / proof-of-innocence and the Tornado Cash sanctions-and-prosecution precedent — which determines whether mirror-pool can ship and who is liable; this is the single most important omission for a fund-custody privacy protocol. (2) The infrastructure/edge/metadata trust surface — relayer/coordinator decentralization, deposit funding-source and Solana fee-payer correlation, and network-layer IP/RPC leakage — where a mathematically perfect mix is routinely undone at its boundaries. (3) ACTIVE and LONGITUDINAL attacks on the mechanism itself — Sybil/n-1 flooding of the k-floor, and multi-round statistical-disclosure/intersection attacks — rather than the single-round passive analysis the fronts emphasized; these two directly void the core k-anonymity claim. (4) Two build-specific inputs: the Groth16 trusted-setup + circom soundness/audit process for the proving stack, and sealed-bid batch-auction prior art (Penumbra ZSwap, CoW) for the novel swap/LP adapter. Separately, the mission's own named adversary — the live Solana MEV/copy-trade bot economy (Jito bundles, sandwichers, GMGN/Photon/Trojan-style wallet trackers) — needs concrete empirical grounding to set the design target and latency budget. Priority before roadmap lock: gap 1 (existential legal), gaps 4 and 7 (attacks that break the guarantee), and gap 6 (defines the success metric); gaps 2/5/8 are must-dos for a credible threat model; gaps 9/10 are design-decision inputs for the proving stack and swap adapter. All references above were web-verified against primary/authoritative sources (SSRN 4563364; Danezis SDA and Serjantov-Dingledine-Danezis blending-attack papers; Penumbra and CoW docs; Helius/DL News Solana MEV reporting; Zcash/Monero viewing-key material); maturity was labeled where it matters (Tornado Cash sanctioned, Penumbra is Cosmos not Solana, Privacy Pools early-production).

- **Compliance-compatible privacy: association sets, proof-of-innocence, and the sanctions/prosecution envelope** — All six fronts are technical anonymity; none addresses the legal-design envelope that decides whether mirror-pool can ship and survive — the sharpest omission for a fund-custody privacy protocol. Primary source: Buterin, Illum, Nadler, Schaer & Soleimani, 'Blockchain Privacy and Regulatory Compliance: Towards a Practical Equilibrium' (2023, SSRN 4563364), which lets users prove via ZK that their funds belong to a vetted 'association set' (or are excluded from a blocklist), creating a separating equilibrium between honest and illicit users, coordinated by Association Set Providers. This is directly implementable as an extra circuit constraint over mirror-pool's existing Poseidon Merkle set and reinforces the 'not about hiding funds' positioning. Equally load-bearing is adversarial legal precedent: OFAC's 2022 Tornado Cash sanctions and the Roman Storm/Semenov prosecutions turned on whether developers/operators controlled the system — bearing directly on how mirror-pool's coordinator is designed and who carries liability. Maturity: Privacy Pools = early-production (0xbow live on Ethereum); Tornado Cash = sanctioned/partly delisted, instructive as a cautionary case.  
  _probe:_ Privacy Pools 0xbow association set provider ZK proof of innocence implementation; OFAC Tornado Cash sanctions Roman Storm verdict developer liability coordinator control
- **Viewing-key / selective-disclosure design (the spec's 'opt-in viewing-key disclosure' is listed but unspecced)** — mirror-pool already commits to opt-in viewing-key disclosure, yet no front covered how to build it safely. Zcash Sapling's split incoming/outgoing viewing keys (IVK/OVK) plus payment-disclosure proofs, versus Monero's single view key, map the design space and its footguns: a shared account-level view key over-discloses, and revocation, rotation, and per-note-vs-per-account granularity each leak differently. This decides exactly what an auditor, tax authority, or counterparty can verify without breaking the anonymity of other round participants, and it is the voluntary complement to gap #1's involuntary association-set disclosure. Relevance to mirror-pool: informs the disclosure module and the note/commitment format (which fields a viewing key must reveal, and whether disclosure is scoped to a single intent or a whole identity). Maturity: Zcash = production; the auditability angle is actively used for regulated/institutional custody.  
  _probe:_ Zcash viewing keys IVK OVK payment disclosure proof design; selective disclosure best practices shielded pool auditability key rotation revocation granularity
- **Relayer/coordinator decentralization, censorship-resistance, and submitter dev-liability** — timing-batching covered correlation and incentives covered paying participants, but neither covered the trust and decentralization of the single entity that signs and submits the round. mirror-pool's coordinator is 'liveness-only' yet can censor/delay and is the natural technical and legal single point of failure (see gap #1's prosecutions). Prior art to mine: Tornado Cash's relayer registry and its capture/censorship failure modes, gas-abstraction/meta-transaction submission patterns, and threshold/committee signing (DKG, plus PBS-style separation of 'who builds the batch' from 'who signs it'). Relevance to mirror-pool: informs whether the vault-signer should be a rotating threshold committee, how submitters are decentralized without any one operator becoming a Tornado-Cash-style target, and how to preserve censorship-resistance under the k-floor. Maturity: relayer model production (Tornado Cash); threshold-submitter designs mostly research/early.

## Appendix B — Per-front practices & fact-check verdicts

### incentives-crowd-depth

The strongest cross-cutting lesson from production privacy systems is that badly-shaped incentives deanonymize faster than they deepen. Tornado Cash's "anonymity mining" grew TVL but paid a public, fixed, per-block reward that let researchers back out exactly how long a deposit sat in a pool; the Tutela study's heuristic suite (of which "TORN mining" is one) flagged ~44% of equal-value deposits as potentially compromised and shrank anonymity sets ~37%. The durable designs instead pay rewards silently — Penumbra accrues staking yield into a delegation-token exchange rate with no per-delegator reward event — and fund liveness through permissionless, bonded, fee-paid relayer/broadcaster markets (Tornado Cash's 300-TORN staked registry; Railgun's ~10%-over-gas non-custodial broadcasters) rather than inflationary deposit rewards. Anti-Sybil economics are existential for an on-chain k-floor: k-anonymity collapses to 1 if a single actor supplies k−1 of the participants, so the floor must be priced with per-intent cost/bonds, and standardized bucketed denominations both block amount-fingerprinting and impose a cost floor. Cold-start is addressed by anchor-tenant seeding (ORE pre-filled Privacy Cash's shielded pool) plus shield-by-default UX (Zcash's Zashi drove shielded supply from ~11% to ~30% in 2025), but seeded intents buy liveness, not genuine anonymity. Finally, Privacy Pools demonstrates that a deep, long-lived honest crowd can be sustained by access/compliance utility with no yield token at all — often the safest incentive is the absence of a reward-farming mechanism. For mirror-pool specifically: keep the k-floor economically Sybil-priced, pay any deferred fee-rewards via silent share appreciation, bucket every PooledAction, and bootstrap with an anchor tenant — but never add a public, per-user, duration- or count-linked reward that reconstructs the member→action mapping the protocol exists to hide.

### timing-batching-correlation

The state of the art in defeating timing/statistical correlation converges on one idea: settle many actions as a single indistinguishable aggregate on a data-independent schedule, and never let the moment or content of an individual action leak before that aggregate is fixed. Penumbra's shielded DEX (Cosmos mainnet since July 2024) is the closest production analog to mirror-pool's synchronized rounds: every swap for a pair in a ~5-second block clears at one uniform price and only the net inter-asset flow is published, so no individual trade is distinguishable within the batch, and swap outputs are minted privately. Threshold-encrypted mempools (Shutter, live on Gnosis Chain; Ferveo; batched threshold encryption, USENIX Security 2024) attack front-running from the other side: proposers must commit to an order while transaction contents are still encrypted, decrypting only after ordering is fixed. The cautionary baseline is Tornado Cash: peer-reviewed analyses (Beres et al., IEEE DAPPS 2021; Tutela, arXiv 2022) show equal denominations alone leave roughly a third of the anonymity set clusterable through timing, gas-price, and address-linkage quasi-identifiers -- an anonymity set is only as strong as its weakest behavioral tell. Mixnet research (Loopix, USENIX 2017; deployed as Nym since 2022) contributes the two remaining primitives: independent exponential per-hop delays that decorrelate input/output timing, and loop/cover traffic that keeps the set full and hides when a real action occurs. Recent attacks (MixMatch, PoPETs 2024; MixFlow) temper those mixnet claims -- deep-learning flow-matching still links some traffic -- so timing defenses must be layered, not relied on singly. For mirror-pool the synthesis is concrete: fire rounds on a fixed cadence once the k-floor is met (not reactively on the marginal intent), standardize action shapes so the batch is a true uniform actor, use pool-generated decoy intents to defend thin rounds, and build an adversarial anonymity-test harness that runs the exact Tornado heuristics against your own rounds.

### measuring-anonymity

Measuring anonymity has a settled 20-year theoretical core plus a fast-moving empirical attack literature; a credible privacy claim needs both. The theory (Serjantov-Danezis and Diaz et al., PET 2002) replaced the naive "count the crowd" anonymity-set size with an information-theoretic effective anonymity set of 2^H over the adversary's posterior distribution — because a non-uniform posterior means the real crowd is smaller than k. Smith's min-entropy / g-leakage (2009+) supplies the conservative companion: the worst-case probability a one-shot adversary correctly attributes an action (Bayes vulnerability), which is exactly the quantity a judge wants bounded at <= 1/k. Empirically, every deployed ZK-mixer (Tornado Cash the canonical case) leaks far below its advertised set: Tutela flagged 8,060 of 28,188 deposits in the 10-ETH pool as compromised; Wang et al. (WWW'23) showed five heuristics materially raise depositor-withdrawer linkage; an early 2025 cross-chain study reported a further FIFO lift but was withdrawn for reference errors, so we do not cite its figure. The breaks are almost never cryptographic — Elliptic and Chainalysis publicly emphasize address labeling/screening, and the academic literature documents the actual behavioral heuristics: address reuse, unique gas price, self-transfers, unique denominations, careless anonymity-mining claims, wallet-software fingerprints, and FIFO timing. The clear directive for an anonymity-test harness: simulate that exact heuristic battery against synthetic rounds, report effective anonymity set (2^H) and per-participant Bayes vulnerability, and treat the on-chain k-floor as a floor on the TRUE (post-heuristic) set, not the nominal count. For mirror-pool's behavioral (non-withdrawal) actions the bar is higher still, because a stake/swap/vote carries shape entropy a fixed-denomination withdrawal mixer never had to standardize.

### solana-privacy-primitives

Solana's binding constraint on any k=hundreds mirror-pool round is not cryptography but raw per-transaction resource ceilings: a 64-account lock limit, a 1,400,000 compute-unit cap, and a 1,232-byte message size (extendable in account count via Address Lookup Tables, but not in CU or lock count). Groth16 verification via alt_bn128 syscalls is cheap enough to be production-viable (~78k-109k CU per proof) but still limits a single instruction to roughly a dozen on-chain verifications, and true cross-transaction atomicity on Solana tops out at 5 transactions via Jito bundles — meaning a k=hundreds round cannot be one atomic unit and must borrow the chunked-sequential-transaction pattern that SPL Stake Pool already uses in production to update hundreds of validator accounts. Light Protocol's ZK Compression is the most mature Solana-native "many accounts, one proof" primitive, but it is explicitly a state-cost/scaling tool, not a privacy tool, and its non-concurrent-write problem (solved via an async nullifier-queue + forester pattern) is a close structural analog to mirror-pool's own Merkle/nullifier design at scale. Two adjacent native privacy primitives are notably immature or offline: Token-2022's confidential transfers have been disabled network-wide since June 2025 after a critical soundness bug, with no confirmed re-enablement found; and Arcium's MPC network, while live on a Solana "Mainnet Alpha" with a real token, is self-described as still permissioned and not fully decentralized. Privacy Cash is the closest live analog to mirror-pool's own cryptographic stack (Circom/Groth16/Anchor) and validates that stack's production-readiness, but it has no synchronized-round or k-floor concept — confirming mirror-pool's round design is a genuine architectural gap on Solana, not a reinvention.

### adversary-chain-analysis

The adversary the bounty describes is not hypothetical: Chainalysis and Elliptic both ship production, Solana-account-model-specific clustering today (linking Token/Stake Accounts back to a System Account), and consumer copy-trading tools (GMGN, Photon-adjacent terminals) already let anyone subscribe to a specific wallet's pending activity. Because Solana is account-based, the classic UTXO common-input-ownership heuristic doesn't transfer directly — the operative signals instead are funding topology (who first funded a wallet), timing correlation, and interaction-sequence fingerprints, which is also exactly the feature set production AI/ML Sybil-detection pipelines (Trusta Labs' graph-community-detection + K-means, LayerZero's 803k-address 2024 sweep) already run at scale. The single most load-bearing precedent for mirror-pool specifically is that ZK shielded pools are not immune to this: peer-reviewed 2025-2026 research deanonymized 21-35% of Tornado Cash withdrawals (and shows comparable leakage in Railgun) purely from timing, address-reuse, and metadata correlation, despite sound Groth16 proofs — proving that k-floor enforcement and round-timing discipline, not proof soundness, are where mirror-pool's real anonymity budget is spent. On the front-running/copy-trading side, Solana's shift of MEV from a deprecated mempool to validator-level shred-streaming and "wide" multi-slot sandwiches is exactly the class of attack a single vault-signed batch transaction structurally removes a target for, and CoW Protocol's live uniform-clearing-price batch auctions are a proven production precedent for extending that same batching logic to future swap/LP adapters. Finally, practitioner and academic sources converge on one design principle worth stating explicitly: naive per-user randomization (uniform-random gas/timing jitter) is itself statistically distinguishable from organic behavior, which is the strongest available argument for mirror-pool's actual approach — collapsing k participants into one uniform on-chain actor — over any "add noise per user" alternative.
